Wilder Clothing

Role: Founder, Head of marketing and product development
Duration: November 2012 - Present
Website: www.wilderclothing.com

A few years ago I went from wearing my New Balance running shoes and sweatpants to school every day to having an obsession (most likely un-healthy) with clothing, style, fashion, and the world that surrounded it. I read every blog, magazine, and tweet I could about it. This persisted for sometime, then after browsing some online store a particular pocket-t struck me. It's price was a little to steep for my highschool student budget. That night I got my Mom (shout out to Daina) to sew a pocket on a black costco t-shirt. That night I wore the shirt out to a party and got some compliments. After the party (and a couple sodas) I was walking home with my friend Scott and we decided let's start a clothing brand.

To start we made a Facebook page to market our shirts. Our merchandise was costco t-shirts that had pockets sewn onto them, they were pretty cool if I do say so my self. We realized that this is not the most efficient way to make money in fashion and so embarked on a mission to create our own cut and sew shirt. I have a family friend that works in fashion for Northern Reflections (to those that don't know this brand it is an old womens brand).

She was able to help us find a factory and pattern maker. Now me and my friend Scott (we were both 16 at this time) organized getting the pattern made, and 100 shirts made. All the material and manufacturing was from and done in Canada. Those 100 shirts were the number one selling item at one of Toronto's "coolest" shops.

Moving on to now, we have made a number of different pieces and it is something that to this day we continue to work on in our spare time. We have sold in shops across Canada and our clothing has been sported by a number of notable figures.

This endeavour has taught me a number of things. I've learned to hustle, as a 16 year old kid it's not easy to convince a shop owner to sell your clothing. I've learned time management and organization. How to sell a product, and create a brand that poeple connect with and enjoy. And lastly i've learned team work, shouts to Scott.

Dalhousie University

Role: Student
Duration: I am a second year student
Website: www.dal.ca

I am currently in my second year of Dalhousie's Commerce Co-op program. This program in my opinion is rather genius. For a students first year they attend school for the typical 2 semesters then go on summer break. When they come back for second year the program changes so that you are in school for 4 months than on co-op for 4 months and do not get a summer. It continues like this untill 4th year when you go back to the standard set up.

This program allows me to learn in a class room for 4 months and then go apply it (and make some money). I take courses like Marketing, Accounting, Finance, Math, and then some electives. School has never been my strength which is one of the reasons I chose the co-op program.

Outside of school I don't do many school sanctioned extracuriculars as I am more interested in charting my own path and doing specific things how and when they interest me. However I have become Bloomberg Terminal certified through the school, taken part in numerous school events, and I am part of the outdoors society.
